    2. QCD Axion search with Gravitational Microlensing

      Axions can form miniclusters in the early universe and still be present in our galactic halo.These axion miniclusters can act as gravitational lenses. When a minicluster passes in front of a distant star, it can cause a temporary increase in the star's brightness, known as a microlensing event.

      A gravitational microlensing event simulation on python with multiple impact parameters

  • Cosmology
    1. Statistical Isotropy Violations in CMB

      BipoSH (Bipolar Spherical Harmonics) implemented in harmonic space provides a general formalism for quantifying the departure from statistical isotropy for a field on a 2D sphere. The related complementary approach in real space can help ferret out the nature of SI violation signals.

    2. Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

      Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) is the process that formed the lightest elements in the early universe. It occurred within the first few minutes after the Big Bang when the universe was hot and dense enough for nuclear reactions to take place. The primary elements produced during BBN were hydrogen, helium, and small amounts of lithium and beryllium. The relative abundances of these light elements provide important evidence for the Big Bang theory and help constrain cosmological models.
